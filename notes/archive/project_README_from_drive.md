## **Research Objectives**

This project investigates how corals regenerate from wounds of different severities. Specifically, we compare a typical microfragmentation wound (ie cutting the tip off) on A. pulchra at ambient (28C) and elevated (31C) temperature over 2 weeks. The study aims to:

• Assess differences in gene expression during critical wound healing and tissue regeneration stages along a single coral fragment

• Determine the effect of wounding x heat on physiological parameters (chlorophyll, zooxanthellae, buoyant weight, PAM, color)

• Characterize the gross and microscopic morphological stages of healing

## **Data Sources & Collection Methods**

### **Experimental Design**

• Coral Samples: 

  \- 208 total A. pulchra fragments (6 cm tall with no obvious branches) from 3 unique thickets were used for physiological response, gene expression, and microscope photography

* \-192 A. pulchra fragments (including the 48 physiological fragments) were used for gene expression analysis and destructive physiological response variables  
  * 48 A. pulchra fragments from 3 thickets (arbitrarily labeled A,C,D) were tracked for non-destructive physiological response variables  
* 16 A. pulchra fragments (thick A and C) were used for daily microscope photography only

• Treatment Groups:

  \- Wounded (yes/no) \- Approximately 1cm was clipped off the growing branch of the nubbin

  \- Temperature (28/31C) \- Temperature was raised 1C/day until treatment temperature was reached. The wound treatment was applied after corals were held at temperature treatments for 7 days

• Standardization:

  \- A caliper was used in conjunction with a band-saw to standardize the wound depth

 \- Aqualogic/Apex system was used to control and monitor temperature continuously
