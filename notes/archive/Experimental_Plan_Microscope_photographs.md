**Experimental Plan: A. pulchra gene expression microscope photographs**

**Objective:** Take a daily time series of wounded A. pulchra under heat stress according to the same protocol for gene expression analysis corals. The purpose of this is to produce a representative series of images that correlates to the wound-healing characterization data taken for the larger (n=192) gene expression x wound x a pulchra experiment.  

**Species:** A. pulchra (n=16)

| Factor | Levels |
| ----- | ----- |
| Temperature | 2 (Ambient (28C), Heated (31C)) |
| Treatment | 1 (Wounded) |
| Timepoints | 22 (sequential days 0-15) |
| Thickets | 2 (A and C) |

**Tank Design:**

* 4 tanks: 2 ambient tanks; 2 heated tanks; 2 thickets (a and c) represented in each tank  
* Within each tank:  
  * 2 fragments from each thicket

**Microscope settings:**

* Each coral is photographed at 1.25 and 3.2 zoom  
* Photo naming scheme   
  * Key: species\_treatment\_coral id\_day\_zoom  
  * Ex: apulchra\_ambient\_12\_D3\_3.2 zoom  
* Aperture and dissection light source adjusted for consistency between images, generally was set to lower end of aperture on microscope and 85-95% light intensity on light source  
* Camera exposure was kept consistent at 169ms, 0 gain  
* Entire microscope mount was raised 2.5cm to accommodate coral height  
* All photos were saved as vsi and converted to 8-bit color images and exported as .tif

**Schedule:**

June 17: Collect 16 A. pulchra (n=8 from thicket A, thicket C), cut to \~6cm tall, and glue to square tiles. Treat for 1 hour with “Worm Exit” (20L water/4 drops worm exit) and blast off any worms with a transfer pipet. Place in mesocosm at ambient temperature (\~28C). See metadata for thicket coordinates. 

June 18-27: Allow corals to acclimate to mesocosm light/water quality conditions

June 27: Split corals so that there are 4 corals in each tank, 2 from each thicket. Begin heat ramp for 2 tanks (n=8 total, n=4 per thicket).

June 30: Hold heat ramp corals at 31C. 28C corals continue at ambient temperature.

July 5: Wound corals by cutting \~1cm off the tip with a band saw. This protocol is identical to the methods used for gene expression study. Photograph corals top down on the microscope at 1.25 and 3.2 zoom daily in the same orientation

July 5-July 20: Photograph corals (n=16) daily. Use the [physio characterization log](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1_6CCmYHGpocI7q9XDzaJHya5NaR-SqArgd24aOSpuoM/edit?usp=sharing) to determine which category of physical characteristics the picture represents. Record yes/no for each of the following parameters:

1. Tissue over wound  
2. Hole in center  
3. Polyp in hole  
4. Wound smoothed  
5. Pigment over wound  
6. Tip exist  
7. Tip extension  
8. New corallites on tip

The goal is to produce a single series of images for heated and ambient healing corals, and not all days may be represented in this image series. This image series may complement the initial [characterization of A. pulchra wound healing](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1wfShEwuEjSP6DPPs6qeFxOPWtqXPlF5Jf_pgiHlPLUo/edit?usp=sharing).
